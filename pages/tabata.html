Quick 20-Minute Tabata Workout

Don’t have time for a butt-kicking workout? Think again. Meet your new go-to workout style, Tabata. It is a type of high intensity training that alternates between 20 seconds of heart-pumping moves and 10 second break periods for eight rounds, totaling a short, explosive four-minutes. Tabata is extremely efficient. It blasts calories, revs up your metabolism and moves so quickly you won’t have time to think about how hard you are working, leaving you sweaty and satisfied. Try this 20-minute, no-equipment workout anytime and anywhere to give your day a sweaty boost.

Warm-Up

It is always key to warm-up before a workout to get your muscles and mind ready to exercise, but for Tabata it is even more important. This workout calls for you to perform each exercise at heart-pumping high intensity, which means your body has to work even harder to keep up.

Make sure to properly warm-up for at least 4 minutes with dynamic moves before you start the Tabata rounds. Some dynamic moves include jumping jacks, high knees, alternating lunges, etc. If you have access to equipment you can also use a jump rope, stationary bike, treadmill or row machine during this time.

Tabata

Follow the Tabata style with 20 seconds active, 10 seconds rest within an AB format that alternates between exercise A and exercise B. Push yourself as hard as possible within these rounds to maximize your results and get the most out of this awesome workout!

Round One

A: 6 Mountain Climbers + 2 Leg Lifts

Hold a plank position, push your knees to your chest alternating each knee 6 times, and then lift one leg straight up and do the same for the other leg. Repeat as many times as possible.

B: Plank Dips

Hold an elbow plank and dip your hips from one side to another.

Round Two

A: Burpees + Alternating Side Leg Raises

Jump straight up, crouch down into a squat and jump back to a plank, add a push-up for an extra challenge and then jump back up. Lift one leg to the side, and then lift the other leg. Repeat as many times as possible.

B: Wide Push-Ups

Place your hands and feet outside of your shoulder width, then hold a plank and lower down and back up for one push-up.

Round Three

A: Reverse Lunge + Skip

Step your right foot back into a backward lunge position then jump your right leg up and repeat. Switch legs after each 20-second round.

B: Triceps Dips

Hold a tabletop position and bend your elbows to give your triceps a nice burn. If you are feeling good, lift your right leg as you bend your elbows and alternate lifting your legs for each dip.

Round Four

A: Skaters

Start in a squat, jump right to land with your left leg closely behind your right ankle and reverse your direction to complete one rep.

B: Spiderman Push-Ups

Start in a plank position as you lower your chest towards the floor bring your left knee to your left elbow and lift back up to complete one push-up, switching knees with each push-up.

Congrats! You are officially a beast for completing this workout. Now cool-down with some deep stretches, and reward yourself with some delicious and nutritious food with a hefty side of water.
